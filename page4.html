Meet Our

Travel Designers Team


Your tedious vacation preparation supports our tenacious tendencies at Canada By Design. We're adventurers, type-A personalities, and doers who have personally journeyed across Canada and Alaska in search of the best experiences. We get a kick out of totally crafting your ideal itinerary and managing the details till your trip runs smoothly. Sure, it's a process filled with precise details and 'what-if' plan b's, but we genuinely enjoy it, and it shows.




Gary Duffy

Gary, who was born in New Zealand and planned to become a sheep farmer, came to Canada when he was 25 years old and fell in love with the country. His job path changed four months later to tourism, with a focus on selling Canada and Alaska. Gary had traveled across Canada more than 80 times by the time he relocated to Canada in April 2000. He continues to travel around Canada and Alaska in pursuit of new and exciting experiences, and his favorite vacation spot is Sonora Resort, which offers wildlife viewing as well as wonderful food and wine.

Favourite Time Of The Year To Travel

My favorite months to travel the Rockies is either May or June, when an abundance of wildlife can be seen feeding near the highways as they wait for the snow to melt on the mountains. Note that from May to mid-June the lakes in the Rockies will still be frozen, so if you want to see the iconic deep blue color I recommend visiting in September.


Clayton Molstad

Clayton grew up in a small town in Northwestern Ontario – the kind of place that gets so much snow in the winter it becomes wise (and sometimes necessary) to take a snowmobile to school. was one of the first Canadian students to do an exchange in the Soviet Union, an experience that set him on a path to explore the world. Having cut his teeth in the telecommunications industry, Clayton was lured into the company on the anticipated challenge of building a business with Gary.

A Special Discovery Made In Canada

Seeing the Kermode bear 'spirit bear' while staying at a remote wilderness lodge off the coast of British Columbia, black bear viewing at the Clayoquot Wilderness Resort, kayaking past grizzlies at Knight Inlet Lodge and polar bear viewing up north in Churchill, Manitoba. Ok… I have a thing for bears!



Leanne Tainsh

Originally from Australia, Leanne has over 15 years of experience working in the travel industry with positions ranging from Finance, Leisure and running Corporate Teams to Business Development Management. Holding a true love for travel, it was after she visited Vancouver, Jasper, Banff and Lake Louise in 2006 that she decided to uproot and move to Vancouver. At any given moment, you can find her planning her next adventure be it a weekend away or longer adventure-style trip.

Best Travel Moment In Canada

I will never forget my first journey to the Rockies! Everything I had read and heard about was true. I traveled by bus to Jasper National Park and enjoyed a week full of amazing scenery, wildlife, skiing, hiking and dining at cozy restaurants. I arrived on the most pristine day to Lake Louise, and it was a surreal 'pinch-me' moment! I could not get enough of the mountains, fresh air and wildlife and I can't wait to do it all over again, next time by rail.



Marzieh Navaei

Originally from Iran, Marzieh came to Canada two years ago to continue her education in MBA at University Canada West. During her residence in Canada, it was the hiking in the BC mountains and swimming at its stunning lakes that helped her stay focused on her path. A lover of local food recipes and various cultures, Marzieh has a special appreciation for Canada and enjoys adventuring, exploring and trying new dishes in her free time. She is excited to be working at Discover Holidays where she can learn more about both Canada and Alaska.

Favourite Place In Canada

Not only is Canada's West Coast my favorite part of the country, but it's an excellent place to surround yourself with wildlife and outdoor activities. Full of fantastic history and culture, there's a lot to learn about the region's Indigenous peoples, and if you're looking for a taste of local cuisine, West Coast cuisine will not let you down!



Charles Liu

Charles came to Canada from China in 2015 to pursue his post-secondary education. After earning his BA in Economics at UVIC in 2020, he realized his passion didn’t lie in the financial industry but rather wanted a job that makes people happy. Thanks to his personal passion for travel, he decided to join the tourism industry and earned his Tourism Marketing Management Diploma at BCIT. In his spare time, Charles loves cooking, drawing, photography, and connecting to nature.

Best Travel Memory In Canada

I traveled alone through Eastern Canada during the summer of 2019, exploring Toronto, Ottawa, Montreal and Quebec City. Among these, Quebec City was my absolute favorite – probably because I haven’t been to Europe, so the feeling there was new and unique.



Ayami Isobe

Born and raised in Japan, Ayami came to Canada 5 years ago to study tourism and graduated with a Tourism Management Degree from Capilano University in North Vancouver. Having traveled to many places around the world, Ayami has a huge appreciation for beautiful scenery and is a true foodie, making a point to try new dishes both in her neighborhood and internationally. She is now a Product Coordinator at Discover Holidays and is excited to explore more of Canada while learning about this amazing country.

One Thing In Canada That Should Be Experienced Atleast Once

Visiting Whistler is an absolute must, regardless of the season. While it's most famous for its winter activities, there are still lots of things to do both in the summer and shoulder seasons. I really like Whistler Village where for its small boutiques and local restaurants!



Shiori Anik

Born in Osaka, Japan, Shiori first came to British Columbia, Canada, in 2015. Her plan was to stay for just one year of school, but after falling in love with the country and its multiculturalism she decided to stay and study Tourism Management and Business Administration. After college, Shiori worked as a Travel Agent and has continued growing her career in tourism with her new role at Discover Holidays. With Vancouver now her long-term home, Shiori loves to go hiking, paddle boarding, snowboarding, camping and adventuring out in nature.

One Thing In Canada That Should Be Experienced Atleast Once

Paddle boarding or kayaking at a lake in Vancouver or even in False Cleek is highly recommended. It's amazing how you can enjoy beautiful urban and mountain views at the same time from the water. I was particularly lucky as I had a chance to see a beaver dam and the family of beavers just two feet in front of me! It’s very relaxing and the perfect way to end my day after work or on the weekend.



Victor Ma

A graduate of the Tourism Marketing program at BCIT, Victor first worked at Landsea Tours and Adventures in Vancouver and Tourism Richmond before joining the team at Canada By Design. Having always had a passion for travel and customer service, Victor is committed to providing an exceptional customer experience to all his clients. Outside of tourism, Victor enjoys trying different craft beers, watching basketball and spending time with his family.

Favourite Place In Canada

My favorite place in Canada would have to be Whistler, British Columbia. Just a two-hour drive from Vancouver, there's endless scenery on the commute along the Sea to Sky Highway and it has so many adventurous activities for both the summer and winter seasons. I especially love all the fantastic food available throughout the village!



Mandy McArthur

Born and raised in North Vancouver, Mandy has long been passionate about travel and tourism. Having traveled across Europe with friends, family and on her own she's experienced many other cultures, and as a coastal girl at heart, some of her favorite destinations in Canada are Tofino, the Sunshine Coast, Kelowna and Victoria. When not working as a Travel Designer, Mandy enjoys camping, road trips and pottery.

Favourite Place In Canada

For me personally, there is no better place to go with friends, family or solo than Tofino on Vancouver Island. I mean, it’s called west coast best coast for a reason! The beaches, hikes and water are all breathtaking, and you can spend hours at the beach surfing and not even notice the time of day. Its a place to escape which is why I love it so much.
